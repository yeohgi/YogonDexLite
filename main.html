Abilities:

Base Stats:

Each Pokemon has their own Base Stat Spread which are not to be confused with EV Spreads which are referred to as Spreads and are covered later. Below is an overview of the significance of each Base Stat.

HP (Hit Points) can be understood as the number of Hit Points a Pokemon can have.

ATK (Attack) can be understood as the stat that dictates how much damage the Pokemons physical attacks will do. Moves which are physical are denoted with a .

DEF (Defense) can be understood as the stat that dictates how much damage your Pokemon will take from others phyical attacks.

SPATK (Special Attack) can be understood as the stat that dictates how much damage the Pokemons special attacks will do. Moves which are special are denoted with a

SPDEF (Special defense) can be understood as the stat that dictates how much damage your Pokemon will take from others special attacks.

SPD (Speed) can be understood as the stat that dictates the order that Pokemon will move during each turn with faster Pokemon moving first within their priority bracket.

Spreads:

EV Spreads:

Effort Value Spreads AKA EV Spreads allow players to invest 508 EVs into any of a Pokemons stats with each stat allowing for a maximum of 252 EVs. EVs make all the difference, Pokemon interactions can completely change based on what stats are invested into. For example, Pokemon who are invested into offensively may be able to threaten OHKOs (One Hit Knock Outs) previously unattainable. Or, Pokemon who are invested into defensively may be able to just barely survive attacks that would previously knock them out, allowing for another turns worth of value.

Natures:

Each Pokemon can have one of many Natures, each nature raising and lowering one of the Pokemons stats. For example, we may find it valueable to lose ATK (Attack) for a raise in SPATK (Special Attack) on a Pokemon that only uses utilizes its SPATK (Special Attack) through special moves.

Moves:

There are 3 different types of moves, Physical Moves, Special Moves, and Status Moves.

Physical Moves are attacking moves that rely on the users ATK stat and recievers DEF stat to calculate damage. Denoted by .

Special Moves are attacking moves that rely on the users SPATK stat and recievers SPDEF stat to calculate damage. Denoted by .

Status Moves are generally non-damaging moves that can inflict some effect on another Pokemon or in some cases the entire battle field. Denoted by .

STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus):

Each move is assigned a type. An important note concerning physical and special moves is a mechanic known as STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus). If a Pokemon uses a move with the same type as itself that moves power will be increased by 1.5x.

Priority:

Sometimes you may find moves are assigned a Priority Level denoted by (priority +X). In Pokemon, when players lock in their moves the order in which each Pokemon moves is determined first by the priority of the moves placing each Pokemon in a priority bracket from -7 to +7, then in each priority bracket speed is used determine which Pokemon will move.

Items:

Items are easy peasy, each Pokemon can hold one item with its own unique effect.

Teammates:

Sometimes Pokemon synergize well with one another. If you see a Pokemon that is almost always paired with another, try to think about why.
